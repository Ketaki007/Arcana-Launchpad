# Purpose

Alpha measures the portion of a portfolio's return that cannot be explained by general market movements or systematic risk factors.

It represents the value generated through investment decisions rather than market exposure alone.

---

# Alpha Generation

Alpha generation is the process of creating returns that consistently outperform an appropriate benchmark after accounting for market risk.

Investment professionals seek alpha by:

- Selecting undervalued securities.
- Constructing better portfolios.
- Identifying market inefficiencies.
- Managing risk more effectively.

Generating sustainable alpha is one of the primary objectives of active investment management.

---

# Why It Matters

Users are not simply interested in whether a portfolio generated positive returns.

They want to understand:

- Did the portfolio outperform expectations?
- Where did the excess return come from?
- Was the performance driven by skill or market conditions?
- Can the strategy continue generating alpha?

---

# Design Implications

Interfaces should:

- Compare portfolio performance against benchmarks.
- Separate market returns from manager skill.
- Explain the drivers of alpha generation.
- Connect alpha to performance attribution and factor exposures.

---

# AI Interpretation

When users analyze performance, avoid presenting returns in isolation.

Where appropriate, distinguish between market-driven returns and alpha generated through investment decisions.
