# Purpose

A benchmark provides the reference point against which portfolio performance is evaluated.

Without a benchmark, portfolio performance lacks meaningful context.

---

# Why It Matters

Investment professionals rarely ask:

"Did my portfolio perform well?"

They ask:

"Did my portfolio perform better than the benchmark?"

Benchmark comparison is fundamental to investment analysis.

---

# Design Implications

Interfaces should:

- present benchmark values alongside portfolio values
- emphasize differences rather than isolated numbers
- preserve comparison context during exploration

---

# AI Interpretation

Whenever performance is displayed, determine whether benchmark comparison should also be surfaced.

Comparison generally provides more value than standalone metrics.
