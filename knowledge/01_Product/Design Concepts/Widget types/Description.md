# Description

## Definition

The Description widget provides foundational reference information about a company, security or financial instrument. It presents descriptive metadata that helps users quickly understand what an organization does, where it operates and how it fits within the broader market.

Unlike analytical widgets that interpret financial performance or market behaviour, the Description widget establishes context by answering fundamental questions about the entity itself.

It acts as the workspace's reference layer.

---

# Purpose

Enterprise users frequently move between hundreds of companies during a single analytical session.

Although experienced analysts may recognise many organisations immediately, unfamiliar companies often require quick contextual understanding before deeper analysis begins.

The Description widget provides that context without forcing users to leave their workspace to search external sources.

Its purpose is to reduce unnecessary context switching while improving analytical confidence.

---

# Mental Model

Think of the Description widget as the cover page of a company's profile.

Before analysing financial statements or market performance, users should understand:

> Who is this company?

> What business are they in?

> Where do they operate?

> Why are they relevant?

The widget establishes identity before analysis begins.

---

# Information Hierarchy

Information should progress from identity to supporting context.

Typical hierarchy:

1. Company name
2. Logo (optional)
3. Business description
4. Industry
5. Sector
6. Headquarters
7. Country
8. Exchange
9. Founded year
10. Employee count
11. Website
12. Key business segments

Reference information should remain concise while allowing access to additional details when needed.

---

# Primary Interactions

Common interactions include:

- Read company overview
- View industry classification
- Open company profile
- Copy company identifiers
- Navigate to external resources
- Expand additional business information

Most interactions are lightweight and intended to support surrounding analytical workflows.

---

# Design Principles

### Establish Context Quickly

Users should understand the nature of the business within a few seconds of viewing the widget.

---

### Prioritize Clarity

Descriptions should be concise, factual and free from marketing language.

The widget exists to inform rather than persuade.

---

### Support Exploration

While the initial presentation should remain compact, users should be able to access richer company information without leaving the analytical workspace.

---

### Remain Secondary

The Description widget provides context rather than driving analysis.

Its visual prominence should remain lower than widgets containing live market or financial data.

---

### Maintain Consistency

Reference information should follow consistent structures across companies, making it easier to compare unfamiliar organisations.

---

# Typical Placement

The Description widget is commonly positioned near the Vertical Quote or Financial Analysis widget where users naturally seek additional context about the selected company.

It often occupies a relatively small area of the workspace because users consult it intermittently rather than continuously.

# Common Mistakes

Displaying excessive historical or corporate information that distracts from current analysis.

Using lengthy narrative descriptions that reduce scanability.

Treating the widget as a marketing profile rather than a factual reference.

Duplicating information already presented in other widgets.

Giving the widget visual prominence equal to primary analytical tools.

---

# AI Interpretation

When generating interfaces:

- Treat the Description widget as contextual reference rather than analytical content.
- Prioritize concise summaries over long-form text.
- Place the widget near other context-establishing widgets.
- Maintain a compact layout that supports quick consultation.
- Avoid unnecessary interactions that interrupt analytical flow.
- Ensure the widget updates automatically when the selected company changes.
