# Page

## Definition

A Page is a logical subdivision within a View that groups together widgets required for a specific stage of analysis.

Pages reduce visual complexity by breaking large workflows into smaller, focused workspaces.

---

# Purpose

As analytical workflows grow, placing every widget on a single canvas becomes overwhelming.

Pages introduce structure without forcing users to leave their current workflow.

---

# Mental Model

Think of Pages as chapters within a notebook.

Each chapter focuses on one topic while remaining part of the larger narrative.

---

# Characteristics

Pages:

- Organise widgets
- Reduce cognitive load
- Support task-based navigation
- Preserve analytical context

---

# Design Principles

Pages should group tasks—not data.

Users should understand why widgets appear together.

# AI Interpretation

When generating layouts:

- Group widgets that naturally support one another.
- Minimise context switching between Pages.
- Keep Pages focused around one analytical objective.
