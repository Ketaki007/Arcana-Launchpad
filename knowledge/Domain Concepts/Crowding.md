# Purpose

Crowding describes situations where many investors hold similar positions or follow similar investment strategies.

Highly crowded positions may become more vulnerable during periods of market stress.

---

# Why It Matters

Investment professionals monitor crowding to understand hidden portfolio risks.

Crowded investments may:

- Increase downside risk.
- Reduce diversification.
- Amplify market movements.
- Create liquidity concerns during rapid sell-offs.

---

# Design Implications

Interfaces should:

- Highlight concentration risks.
- Surface unusual crowding levels.
- Connect crowding to portfolio risk.
- Allow comparison across portfolios and sectors.

---

# AI Interpretation

Crowding should be presented as contextual risk information rather than an isolated metric.

Where appropriate, relate crowding to portfolio composition and overall risk.
