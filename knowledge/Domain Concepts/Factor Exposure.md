# Purpose

Factor exposure measures how strongly a portfolio is influenced by systematic market factors such as value, growth, momentum, size or volatility.

It helps explain why a portfolio behaves the way it does under different market conditions.

---

# Why It Matters

Investment professionals use factor exposures to:

- Understand portfolio behavior.
- Identify unintended risks.
- Explain performance.
- Improve diversification.
- Evaluate investment strategies.

---

# Design Implications

Interfaces should:

- Present factor exposures alongside portfolio performance.
- Support comparison across portfolios and benchmarks.
- Show changes in exposure over time.
- Connect factor exposure to risk and performance attribution.

---

# AI Interpretation

Factor exposure should rarely be presented alone.

Whenever possible, relate it to performance, portfolio construction and investment risk.

Help users understand not only the exposure itself but also its impact.
