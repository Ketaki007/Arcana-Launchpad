# Product Philosophy

## Purpose

Arcana is an investment intelligence platform that helps institutional investors transform complex portfolio and market data into actionable insights.

The platform exists to support informed decision-making by making financial data easier to understand, compare and act upon.

Rather than simply presenting information, Arcana helps users understand **why** outcomes occur and what actions they can take next.

---

# Product Mission

Enable investment professionals to:

- Understand portfolio performance
- Investigate drivers behind performance
- Analyze portfolio risk and exposures
- Compare investment opportunities
- Evaluate scenarios with confidence
- Make informed investment decisions backed by evidence

---

# Nature of Work

Arcana primarily supports **analytical decision-making**.

Typical user activities include:

- Comparing portfolios
- Investigating anomalies
- Understanding performance attribution
- Evaluating risk
- Exploring market exposures
- Running scenario analysis
- Monitoring investment opportunities

The interface should therefore prioritize understanding and reasoning over task completion speed.

---

# Product Values

## Clarity over Complexity

Complex financial information should become easier to interpret without hiding important details.

### Design Implications

- Prioritize information hierarchy.
- Reveal complexity progressively.
- Avoid unnecessary visual decoration.
- Use familiar interaction patterns.

---

## Evidence before Recommendation

Insights should always be supported by the data that generated them.

### Design Implications

- Surface contributing factors.
- Explain recommendations whenever possible.
- Preserve transparency throughout analytical workflows.

---

## Comparison over Memorization

Users frequently compare portfolios, securities and scenarios.

Interfaces should reduce the need to remember information across multiple views.

### Design Implications

- Support side-by-side comparison.
- Preserve context while exploring.
- Minimize unnecessary navigation.

---

## Confidence over Speed

Helping users make the correct investment decision is more valuable than minimizing clicks.

### Design Implications

- Avoid hiding critical information.
- Prioritize understanding before action.
- Encourage deliberate decision-making for high-impact workflows.

---

## Transparency

Users should understand not only **what** happened but also **why** it happened.

### Design Implications

- Expose assumptions when possible.
- Explain calculations and metrics.
- Reveal relationships between data rather than presenting isolated numbers.

---

# AI Interpretation

When generating interfaces for Arcana, prioritize:

- Analytical workflows
- Information hierarchy
- Scanability
- Comparison
- Context preservation
- Progressive disclosure
- Trust through transparency
- dark mode as the default analytical experience

Avoid:

- Decorative layouts
- Consumer-style interfaces
- Excessive animations
- Hiding important information behind interactions
- Optimizing purely for click reduction

When multiple interface solutions are possible, prefer the option that improves user understanding rather than visual simplicity.
