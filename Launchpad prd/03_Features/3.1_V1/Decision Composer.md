# Decision Composer

## Overview

Financial analysis is not just about gathering information—it is about transforming information into a well-reasoned investment thesis.

Throughout an investigation, analysts review financial statements, monitor price movements, compare competitors, read earnings transcripts, collect news and record observations. While modern analytical platforms provide access to vast amounts of data, they provide relatively little support in connecting these pieces into a coherent narrative.

Decision Composer introduces an AI-assisted layer that helps analysts synthesize information collected across their workspace into a structured draft. Rather than replacing analytical judgement, it acts as a collaborative thinking partner that organizes evidence, surfaces connections and accelerates the transition from research to decision-making.

The feature is designed to augment reasoning—not automate it.

---

## Why This Feature Matters

Professional analysts spend a significant portion of their time moving between information gathering and information synthesis.

After reviewing multiple widgets, analysts often pause to answer questions such as:

- What have I learned so far?
- Which findings support my hypothesis?
- What evidence contradicts it?
- What should I investigate next?
- Am I missing any important information?

Answering these questions requires manually connecting information spread across multiple widgets and personal notes.

As investigations become larger and span multiple sessions, maintaining this mental model becomes increasingly difficult.

Decision Composer reduces this cognitive effort by helping analysts organize the information they have already collected into a structured draft that can be reviewed, refined and challenged.

---

## Design Objectives

Decision Composer is guided by four principles.

### Organize, Don't Decide

The feature should help structure information rather than recommend investment actions.

Its role is to improve clarity, not replace professional judgement.

---

### Ground Every Insight

Every statement generated by Decision Composer should be traceable to information already present within the workspace.

Users should always understand where a conclusion originated.

Transparency builds trust and allows analysts to validate the underlying evidence.

---

### Encourage Critical Thinking

Rather than presenting a polished answer, the feature should encourage users to question assumptions, explore alternative viewpoints and identify missing evidence.

The goal is to strengthen analysis, not prematurely conclude it.

---

### Fit Naturally into Existing Workflows

Decision Composer should become a natural extension of the analytical process.

Users should invoke it when they feel ready to pause, reflect or prepare a recommendation—not as a mandatory step in every investigation.

---

## The Proposed Experience

At any point during an investigation, users can launch Decision Composer from the workspace toolbar.

The feature reviews the current analytical context, including:

- Active widgets
- Selected securities
- Financial metrics
- News articles
- User notes
- Previous workspace observations

Rather than generating a generic summary, Decision Composer organizes the available information into a structured narrative.

A typical draft may include:

### Investigation Summary

A concise overview of the current research objective.

---

### Key Findings

Important observations collected throughout the investigation.

Example:

- Revenue growth accelerated over the previous three quarters.
- Gross margins continue to improve.
- Recent product launches exceeded market expectations.

---

### Supporting Evidence

Links each finding back to the widgets or notes from which it was derived.

Users can immediately navigate to the original source for validation.

---

### Open Questions

Highlights areas where additional investigation may be valuable.

Examples include:

- Competitor comparison not yet completed.
- Insider ownership not reviewed.
- Recent guidance changes require validation.

---

### Draft Investment Thesis

Produces a structured narrative that users can edit, expand or discard.

The draft serves as a starting point rather than a final recommendation.

---

## Key Capabilities

Decision Composer introduces several capabilities that support analytical reasoning.

### Evidence Synthesis

Connects information from multiple widgets into a coherent narrative without requiring users to manually consolidate every observation.

---

### Traceable References

Every generated statement includes links back to the underlying data source, helping users understand how each insight was formed.

This reinforces trust while making validation effortless.

---

### Gap Identification

Recognizes areas where the available information appears incomplete and encourages further investigation before conclusions are reached.

---

### Editable Drafts

Generated content is always editable.

Analysts remain free to rewrite, reorganize or reject any suggestion.

The output belongs to the user—not the system.

---

### Export and Sharing

The completed draft can be copied into research reports, meeting notes or shared with colleagues as the starting point for collaborative discussion.

## User Journey

An equity analyst has spent the morning researching NVIDIA ahead of its quarterly earnings announcement.

Their workspace includes financial analysis, relative valuation, price history, recent news and several personal notes collected throughout the investigation.

Before preparing a recommendation, they launch Decision Composer.

Within a few moments, Launchpad generates a structured draft.

The summary identifies accelerating revenue growth, improving margins and positive market sentiment while linking each observation back to the relevant widget.

It also highlights that no competitor comparison has been completed and suggests reviewing AMD before finalizing the investment thesis.

The analyst edits the draft, removes one observation they disagree with and expands another using their own expertise.

What previously required manually assembling information from multiple sources now begins with an organized first draft that remains fully transparent and entirely under the analyst's control.

---

## Design Considerations

Decision Composer is intentionally positioned as a thinking companion rather than an autonomous analyst.

The feature should never imply certainty where uncertainty exists, nor should it produce recommendations that cannot be traced back to supporting evidence.

Transparency is therefore fundamental.

Users should always be able to understand *why* something appears in the draft and immediately inspect the original source.

Equally important is preserving the analyst's agency.

The generated narrative should invite refinement, disagreement and further investigation. Analysts should feel comfortable deleting sections, rewriting conclusions or ignoring suggestions altogether.

By emphasizing collaboration over automation, Decision Composer strengthens professional judgement instead of competing with it.

---

## Measuring Success

Decision Composer should reduce the effort required to transform research into a structured analytical narrative.

Success can be measured through indicators such as:

- Reduced time spent preparing investment summaries.
- Increased use of generated drafts during investigations.
- High rates of user edits and refinements, indicating active collaboration rather than passive acceptance.
- Positive feedback regarding transparency and trustworthiness.
- Increased confidence in resuming and concluding complex investigations.

Ultimately, success is not measured by how often users accept the generated output unchanged, but by whether it helps them think more clearly and communicate their reasoning more effectively.

---

## Looking Ahead

Decision Composer represents the first step towards AI-assisted financial reasoning within Launchpad.

Future iterations could incorporate historical investigations, organizational knowledge and collaborative feedback to produce richer, more context-aware drafts. Analysts could compare current findings with previous research, revisit past investment theses or understand how conclusions evolved over time.

Even as these capabilities expand, the core philosophy remains unchanged: AI should amplify human expertise, not replace it.

Decision Composer is designed to help analysts arrive at better-informed decisions by making the process of thinking, connecting and communicating evidence significantly easier.
